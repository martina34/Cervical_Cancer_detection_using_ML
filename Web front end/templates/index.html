Facts About Cervical Cancer

  • Human papillomavirus (HPV) is the main cause of cervical cancer
  • There are more than 3,000 cases of cervical cancer in the UK per year
  • 99.8% of UK cervical cancer cases are preventable
  • All women should begin screening at age 21

Today Is Your Day

Cervical cancer is one of the most common types of cancer occurring in women worldwide. It affects the cervix, the lower part of the uterus located at the top of the vagina. It is mainly caused by human papillomavirus (HPV) infection, which is transmitted sexually, and it usually develops over several years.
